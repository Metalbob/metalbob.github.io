Professional work!

My carreer started as a software engineer at Protectic which is a company that sells talents to other company in the industry, aeronotics and space sector. My first mission was for Thales. I worked on a sky space surveillance software. I also did the porting of the software to newer technologies.

About

I'm interested in game development since I'm young. I was asking myself about how the games I played on my sister's SNES were created. While looking over the internet, I discovered programming! At this time I was just following tutorials. But then, at highschool, I created my first programs on a programmable calculator (the TI-94).

Then I started to learn C and made my first games using the SDL framework. It was at this moment I knew that it will be my dream job!

I got accepted at ISART Digital, a school specialised in game development and animation movies. I learnt a lot in this school, from gameplay to engine programming, making it from scratch or using game engine such as Unity or Unreal Engine 4.

To validate my diploma, I had to do 2 internships. I made both of them at Manzalab, a company making serious games for other companies. There, I improved my Unity skills on the field. I worked on an helicopter simulation game for a laboratory which was studying brain reactions and learning to some event happening while playing. I worked directly with the scientists, an international team, using technologies such as the serial port. It was fun to work on this project.

2 years after graduating, I moved to Virtual Room and worked with a team made of talented people on lots of VR projects! The best working experience I had! I learnt more about UE4, improving my knowledges about networking, physics and technical constraint of the VR support. And also, as I became the lead of the programmers team, I learnt about the technologies surrounding the game development such as management and tools setup (or Perforce migration from a linux to a windows server. This is a warning: don't do that, it's painful!)
